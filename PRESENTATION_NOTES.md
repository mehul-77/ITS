# Presentation Notes

## Recommended Presentation Flow

1. Start with the transport problem in India:
   congestion, rapid urban growth, infrastructure imbalance, and the need for data-driven ITS planning.

2. Explain the project idea:
   combine satellite-image reading and road-network analytics into one automated tool.

3. Highlight the ITS core topics covered:
   road hierarchy, connectivity, intersections, density, corridor screening, and ITS readiness.

4. Show the revamped workflow:
   image preprocessing -> road extraction -> feature extraction -> map analytics -> dashboard outputs.

5. Demonstrate why the new system is stronger:
   the pipeline now uses hybrid ridge, surface, and edge cues instead of a single classical method.

6. Connect the project to Indian case studies:
   Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, and Surat.

7. End with outcomes and future scope:
   smart-city use cases, traffic monitoring support, route planning extensions, and more accurate geospatial evaluation.

## Slide Suggestions

- Slide 1: Title and problem statement
- Slide 2: Background and motivation in the Indian context
- Slide 3: Objectives
- Slide 4: Data sources and study locations
- Slide 5: Methodology pipeline
- Slide 6: Automated tools and dashboard screenshots
- Slide 7: Feature extraction outputs from satellite imagery
- Slide 8: ITS metrics and readiness analysis
- Slide 9: Expected outcomes and milestones
- Slide 10: Resources, budget, and future work

## Faculty-Facing Emphasis

- Keep the project framed as practical, low-cost, and scalable.
- Stress that the work is not only image processing but also transportation analytics.
- Mention that the system is designed for first-review maturity now and can later expand into geospatially accurate, benchmarked ITS research.
